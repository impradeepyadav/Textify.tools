How to use these tools (it's not rocket science)

You really don't need instructions, but since you're here, here's the 4‑step flow most people follow:

1. Paste or type

Dump your messy JSON, a blog draft, a phone number, or anything else. The box accepts whatever you throw at it.

2. Tweak a setting (if you feel like it)

Maybe you want uppercase instead of lowercase, or a different delimiter. It's optional — the tool already works before you touch anything.

3. Watch the result appear

Hit the button (or sometimes just watch it auto‑update) and the output is there instantly. No server calls, just your browser doing the work.

4. Grab it and go

Copy to clipboard, download as a file, or share a link. The output is yours to do whatever you want with.

Why I don't want your data (honestly)

Storing user data is a huge headache. Servers cost money, GDPR is scary, and hackers are always poking around. By keeping every tool in your browser, I avoid all that. Your text never leaves your device. I can't lose what I never had.

When you use the JSON formatter, that ugly payload stays inside your own browser's memory. Close the tab, and poof — it's gone. I don't even keep a log of you visiting (well, Google Analytics counts page views, but that's anonymous).

If a government ever asks for your data, I'll happily show them an empty folder. That's privacy by design, not just a fancy policy.

Let's talk about privacy for a sec

I built Textify.tools after getting frustrated with sites that demanded signups, pushed giant ads, or made me wonder where my data was going. Here's what you can count on:

  • Zero server processing — All the magic happens in your browser using JavaScript. There is no backend for tool processing.
  • No account, ever — No email, no password, no social login. Just open the page and go.
  • No tracking of your text — I use Google Analytics to see anonymous page views, but I never track what you type.
  • Works offline — Once a tool page loads, you can disconnect your internet and it'll still work. Bookmark your favorites for flights or remote areas.
