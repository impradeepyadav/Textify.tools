Why Use a Word Frequency Counter?

Word frequency analysis is useful for many scenarios:

  • SEO & Content Optimization — Identify overused keywords or find the most relevant terms in your content.
  • Plagiarism Detection — Spot unusual word repetition patterns.
  • Academic Writing — Avoid repetitive language and improve vocabulary variety.
  • Social Media Analysis — Understand which words or hashtags appear most frequently in posts or comments.
  • Language Learning — Analyze which words appear most often in a foreign language text to focus learning on frequent vocabulary.

About Stop Words

The word frequency counter includes a built‑in list of over 150 common English stop words (e.g., "a", "an", "the", "of", "to", "and", "for", "with"). Enabling the "Filter stop words" option will remove these words from the analysis, allowing you to focus on meaningful, content‑bearing words. You can also set a minimum word length to ignore very short words.

Privacy & Security

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the word frequency counter?
The tool splits text by spaces and punctuation, then counts each occurrence exactly. With case‑sensitivity off (default), "Hello" and "hello" are counted as the same word.
What are stop words?
Stop words are common words (such as "the", "and", "of", "to") that are often filtered out in text analysis because they add little meaning. You can enable or disable this filter as needed.
Does it work on mobile?
Yes — the page is fully responsive and works on iPhones, Android devices, and tablets.
Is my text private?
Absolutely. All processing happens directly in your browser — no data leaves your device.
