What Is Glitch (Zalgo-Style) Text?

You may have noticed text that appears distorted or layered with unusual symbols above and below the letters. This style is commonly known as glitch or Zalgo-style text. It is created by adding special Unicode combining characters to standard letters, which results in a visually distorted effect. Textify's Glitch Mode automatically applies these characters, making it useful for creative typography, visual experiments, and decorative text styles.

You can create simple ASCII art or geometric patterns by repeating symbols with precise separators. Try these combos:

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Text not pasting on mobile? Some apps limit how much you can paste at once. Break the text into smaller chunks by generating with a lower repeat count (e.g., 500 instead of 5,000) and pasting in sections.
  • App freezing with large text? Very long texts (over 50,000 characters) can slow down messaging apps. Use our chunked processing (it auto‑activates above 50,000 reps) and consider downloading the .txt file to copy in portions.
  • Spam filters? If you're sending many messages, stick to one long message with new‑line separators instead of dozens of individual messages. This reduces the chance of being flagged.
  • Characters look weird? Some fonts (like flip or glitch) use special Unicode characters that may not display on older devices. Test with a small count first, then go big.
