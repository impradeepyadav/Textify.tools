What Is Keyword Density?

Keyword density is the percentage of times a keyword or phrase appears in your text compared to the total word count. It is calculated as: (Keyword Occurrences ÷ Total Words) × 100. For example, if your 500‑word article uses the phrase "SEO tips" 10 times, the density is 2% — right in the sweet spot.

Search engines use keyword density (along with many other signals) to understand what a page is about. A healthy density — generally 1% to 3% — shows relevance without over‑optimisation. Below 0.5% may indicate thin coverage, while above 4% risks triggering keyword‑stuffing signals that can hurt rankings.

Most SEO professionals recommend 1% to 3%. For a 1,000‑word article, that means your primary keyword appears roughly 10‑30 times. However, focus on natural writing — modern search engines value comprehensive topic coverage over mechanical density targets.
Can I check phrases, not just single words?

Stop words are common words like "a", "the", "in", "on", "is". Filtering them out ensures your analysis highlights meaningful keywords instead of cluttering the results with high‑frequency filler words.
