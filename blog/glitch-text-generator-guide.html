Glitch & Zalgo Text: Cool Effects Explained

📅 April 18, 2026

You have probably seen text that looks corrupted – letters with strange marks crawling above and below, almost as if the text is haunted. This style is commonly called glitch text or Zalgo text. It's widely used in memes, horror stories, game titles, and social media to grab attention. In this guide, we'll explain what glitch text actually is, how it works, and how you can generate it instantly with the Textify Text Repeater.

What is Zalgo / Glitch Text?

Zalgo text (named after an internet creepypasta character) is regular text combined with Unicode combining characters. Unicode includes a set of diacritical marks (like accents) that can stack on top of a letter. When you add many of these marks to a single character, they pile up, creating a distorted, glitchy appearance. For example, the letter "T" can become "T̷̄̒̈̋" when several combining marks are attached.

These combining characters are perfectly valid Unicode and display correctly in most modern browsers and apps. They don't damage your text – they are simply visual decorations.

How Does It Work?

The magic lies in the Unicode block Combining Diacritical Marks (U+0300–U+036F). There are 112 such marks, including things like combining grave accent, combining tilde, combining hook, etc. When you append several of these to a base character, they stack vertically, creating the glitch effect. The more marks you add, the more chaotic the text looks.

Textify's Glitch Mode does exactly this – it randomly picks a few combining marks for each character and attaches them, resulting in a unique distortion every time.

Where to Use Glitch Text

Glitch text is surprisingly versatile. Here are some creative uses:

  • Social media bios: Make your Twitter or Instagram bio stand out with a glitchy name.
  • Gaming usernames: Many gamers use Zalgo text to create spooky in‑game names.
  • Horror / mystery content: Perfect for storytelling, creepypasta, or Halloween posts.
  • Graphic design: Use as a design element in posters or thumbnails.
  • Pranks: Send a "corrupted" message to a friend (just don't overdo it!).

Is Glitch Text Safe to Use?

Absolutely. The text you generate is standard Unicode and will not harm any device or account. However, a few things to keep in mind:

  • Some older systems or fonts may not render combining marks correctly, showing empty boxes instead.
  • Very long chains of combining marks can exceed character limits on platforms like Twitter.
  • Excessive use might make your text unreadable – use it sparingly for maximum impact.

Zalgo Text vs. Other Text Effects

Unlike font styles (bold, italic, etc.) that replace characters with mathematical bold/italic symbols, glitch text adds to the existing character. This means you can combine it with other font transformations. For instance, take a cursive word and then apply glitch – the result is a cursive word with creepy marks. The possibilities are endless.
