What Do Vowels and Consonants Include?

This tool counts vowels as the letters: A, E, I, O, U (both uppercase and lowercase). All other letters (B, C, D, F, G, H, J, K, L, M, N, P, Q, R, S, T, V, W, X, Y, Z) are counted as consonants. The letter 'Y' is counted as a consonant — while 'Y' can sometimes act as a vowel (e.g., in "sky"), the standard classification treats it as a consonant in most text analysis contexts.

The tool also shows the frequency of each vowel and consonant individually, making it easy to spot which letters appear most often in your text.

Why Use a Vowel & Consonant Counter?

Analysing vowel and consonant distribution is useful for many scenarios:

  • Writers & Poets – Understand the rhythm and phonetics of your writing; vowels often create softer sounds, while consonants add sharpness.
  • Students & Linguists – Analyse language patterns, conduct linguistic research, or complete assignments on letter frequency.
  • Educators – Use the tool in classrooms to help students learn about letter types and phonetics.
  • Cryptography Enthusiasts – Letter frequency analysis is a fundamental technique in code‑breaking.
  • Content Creators – Improve the readability and flow of your text by balancing vowel and consonant usage.

Privacy & Security

All analysis happens locally in your browser. Your text is never uploaded to any server — you can use this tool with complete peace of mind for any document, including sensitive or proprietary content.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the vowel/consonant counter?
The tool is 100% accurate for standard English letters. It only counts A, E, I, O, U (both cases) as vowels; all other letters are counted as consonants. Numbers, spaces, punctuation, and special characters are ignored.
Does it work on mobile?
Yes — the page is fully responsive and works on iPhones, Android devices, and tablets.
Is my text private?
Absolutely. All processing happens directly in your browser — no data leaves your device.
Does it treat 'Y' as a vowel?
'Y' is always counted as a consonant in this tool. While 'Y' can sometimes act as a vowel in specific contexts (like "myth" or "sky"), the standard classification in letter‑frequency analysis treats it as a consonant.
