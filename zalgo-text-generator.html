What is Zalgo Text?

Zalgo text (also known as "glitch" or "corrupted" text) is created by adding special Unicode combining characters to regular letters. These combining marks stack above, below, or through the base character, creating a distorted, creepy appearance that looks like text from a horror movie or a glitched computer.

The name "Zalgo" comes from an internet creepypasta character, and the style is often used in horror memes, game mods, social media pranks, and creepy stories. Because it's built entirely with standard Unicode, Zalgo text works across most modern platforms without any special fonts or software.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Zalgo text safe to use?
Yes. The text generated is standard Unicode and will not damage any system. However, some older apps may not render combining marks correctly, and extremely long strings of combining marks could cause performance issues in certain software.
Why does some text not work?
Some characters (like emojis or very rare Unicode symbols) may not accept combining marks. The generator will preserve them but won't add Zalgo effects.
Where can I use Zalgo text?
Most modern apps support Zalgo text, including Discord, Twitter, Reddit, WhatsApp, and Instagram. It's widely used for usernames, bios, and spooky messages.
Is it really private?
Absolutely. All Zalgo generation happens locally in your browser. No data is sent to any server – what you type stays on your device.
