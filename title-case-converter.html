Understanding Title Case Styles

Default Title Case: Simply capitalises the first letter of every word.

APA Style: Capitalise first word, all nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and subordinating conjunctions (e.g., “after”, “although”, “because”). Lowercase articles, coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet), and prepositions of any length — unless they are the first or last word.

Chicago Style: Similar to APA, but Chicago lowercases all prepositions regardless of length. Articles and coordinating conjunctions are also lowercased (unless first/last word).

MLA Style: Capitalise the first and last word and all principal words (nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, subordinating conjunctions). Lowercase articles, coordinating conjunctions, and prepositions — again, unless they are the first or last word.

AP Style: Capitalise all words with four or more letters. Also capitalise the first and last words. All other words (articles, conjunctions, short prepositions) are lowercased.
