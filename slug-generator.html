What is a URL Slug?

A slug is the part of a URL that identifies a particular page in a readable way. For example, in example.com/blog/how-to-make-coffee, the slug is how‑to‑make‑coffee. Slugs help search engines and users understand the page content.

How This Tool Works

The Slug Generator converts your text to lowercase, removes special characters, and replaces spaces with hyphens. Multiple hyphens become single ones, and leading/trailing hyphens are removed. In bulk mode, each non‑empty line is processed individually — perfect for batch‑generating slugs for multiple pages or blog posts.

Tips for Great Slugs

  • Keep it short – aim for 3‑5 words.
  • Use hyphens to separate words (never underscores).
  • Avoid special characters like &, %, or punctuation.
  • Include keywords that describe the page content.
