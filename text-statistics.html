Text Statistics – Advanced Text Analyzer

Get in‑depth analysis of your text: word count, unique words, readability, lexical density, reading time, character counts, and much more. All processing happens locally in your browser — completely private.

How to Use the Text Statistics Tool

This tool provides a comprehensive analysis of your text. Here's what each metric means:

  • Word Count & Character Count: Total words and characters (with options to exclude spaces, numbers, or punctuation).
  • Unique Words & Vocabulary Richness: Number of distinct words and the ratio of unique words to total words (higher values indicate richer vocabulary).
  • Sentence & Paragraph Count: Automatically detected using punctuation and line breaks.
  • Reading & Speaking Time: Based on standard reading speed (200–250 WPM) and speaking speed (130 WPM).
  • Readability Score & Lexical Density: Approximate reading ease (higher score = easier to read) and the percentage of content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs).
  • Most Common Words: A quick overview of the top 5 most frequent words.

All calculations are performed instantly in your browser — no data is sent to any server.

Why Use an Advanced Text Statistics Tool?

Whether you're a writer, editor, student, or content creator, detailed text statistics help you:

  • Improve readability: Adjust your writing to match your target audience's reading level.
  • Avoid repetition: Spot overused words or phrases that might make your text monotonous.
  • Meet content requirements: Ensure your text meets specific length or vocabulary targets.
  • Understand your style: Gain insights into your unique writing patterns and lexical preferences.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the readability score?
The readability score is an estimate based on the Flesch Reading Ease formula, which considers average word length and average sentence length. It works best for English text and provides a general indication of difficulty.
What does "Lexical Density" mean?
Lexical density is the percentage of content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) in your text. A higher density often indicates more information‑dense, complex writing, while lower density suggests more grammatical filler words.
Can I analyze longer documents?
Yes, the tool can handle very large documents efficiently. Performance may vary based on your device, but it generally works well for texts up to several hundred thousand characters.
Does it work on mobile?
Absolutely! The page is fully responsive and works on all devices including iPhones, Android phones, and tablets.
