    Why Use Textify Word Counter?

    Most word counters are slow, clunky, or require you to upload your text to a server. Our tool runs entirely in your browser — you get instant results without sacrificing privacy. Plus, you get readability scores, syllable count, lexical density, and much more that you won't find elsewhere.

    • Live statistics – no need to click a button. Every stat updates as you type or paste.
    • Complete privacy – your text stays on your device. We don’t see it, we don’t store it.
    • Readability scores – Flesch Reading Ease and Flesch‑Kincaid Grade Level shown numerically.
    • Text history – save multiple versions locally and compare them anytime.
    • Works offline – once loaded, no internet needed. All processing stays local.

We count any sequence of non‑whitespace characters as a word, just like most word counters. Differences usually happen because of footnotes, text boxes, or special formatting that those editors count separately. Our counter gives you a clean, plain‑text count that matches what you'd see if you pasted into Notepad first. If you need exact parity with MS Word, strip all hidden content and paste only the body text — the numbers will align.

The readability scores and syllable counter are tuned for English, so they'll be less accurate for other languages, but the raw counts (words, characters, sentences) are rock‑solid for almost any script. If you're working with a language that doesn't use spaces (like Chinese), the character count is your best friend.

Seriously though, we built this to be lightweight, so you're unlikely to hit a wall.

    We calculate two classic scores: Flesch Reading Ease (higher = easier) and Flesch‑Kincaid Grade Level (the US school grade needed to understand the text). These are based on average sentence length and syllables per word. We use a syllable‑counting heuristic that's about 95% accurate for English — good enough for most purposes. For academic work, these scores are widely accepted as quick readability indicators. If you need pharmaceutical‑grade accuracy, you'd want a dedicated linguistics tool, but for essays, blog posts, and reports, this is exactly what writing centres use.
