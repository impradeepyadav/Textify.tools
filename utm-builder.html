What Are UTM Parameters?

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are tags added to the end of a URL. They let analytics tools like Google Analytics 4 (GA4) track exactly where your website visitors are coming from. Without UTMs, all social traffic might show as "direct / none" and you'll miss which campaigns are working.

Pro Tip: Keep your UTMs consistent. Use lowercase, replace spaces with underscores or dashes, and agree on a naming convention with your team before you launch a campaign.

UTM Parameter Guide

  • utm_source: Where the traffic originates (e.g., facebook, twitter, newsletter).
  • utm_medium: The marketing medium (e.g., cpc, social, email, banner).
  • utm_campaign: The specific campaign name (e.g., black_friday, summer_sale).
  • utm_term (optional): Used for paid search to track keywords.
  • utm_content (optional): Used to differentiate between two similar links within the same campaign.

Best Practices for UTM Naming

  • Be consistent with case – always lowercase to avoid duplicate entries in reports.
  • Use underscores or hyphens instead of spaces (our tool handles spaces automatically, but it's good practice).
  • Don't use UTMs for internal links – only for external campaigns.
  • Keep a spreadsheet of all UTMs so your team can see which tags have already been used.
  • Test your URLs – paste them into your browser to make sure they work before you send them out.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I leave optional fields blank?
That's fine. Only source, medium, and campaign name are required. Empty optional fields are simply ignored.
Does this tool work offline?
Yes, once the page loads, the UTM generation runs entirely in your browser. No internet connection is needed.
Will this tool track my data?
No. Everything is processed locally. We never see your URLs or campaign information.
Can I use UTMs on social media?
Absolutely. UTMs are perfect for tracking Facebook posts, Instagram bio links, tweets, and even LinkedIn articles.
Do UTMs affect SEO?
No. Search engines ignore UTM parameters. They're only used by analytics tools and don't harm your rankings.
