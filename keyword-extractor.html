Why use this Keyword Extractor?

Whether you're optimizing a blog post for search engines, analyzing a competitor's content, or just curious about the most frequent terms in a document, a keyword extractor saves you from manually counting words. It filters out common words (stop words), enforces a minimum length, and sorts by frequency, giving you a clean list of what matters most.

  • Stop word removal – automatically skips words like "the", "and", "is" using a built‑in English list, plus you can add custom ones.
  • Adjustable word length – ignore short words (2‑5 characters) to focus on meaningful terms.
  • Case‑insensitive matching – treat "Keyword" and "keyword" as the same (on by default).
  • Top N filter – display only the most frequent keywords, up to 100, so you don't get overwhelmed by noise.

How to extract keywords (step‑by‑step)

1. Paste your text

Copy any article, blog post, or text snippet and drop it into the left box.

2. Tweak the options

Decide if you want to remove stop words, set a minimum word length, and choose how many keywords to display. You can also add custom words to ignore.

3. Get the keywords

The extracted list appears instantly on the right, sorted by frequency. Each keyword shows how many times it appeared.

4. Copy or clear

Click "Copy List" to save the keywords for your SEO sheet, or "Clear All" to start a fresh analysis.

Common use cases

  • SEO keyword research: Quickly find potential target keywords from a competitor's article.
  • Content auditing: Check if your most important terms appear enough times in your draft.
  • Academic analysis: Identify the dominant themes in a research paper or book chapter.
  • Tag generation: Auto‑generate tags or categories for a blog post based on its content.
  • Data cleaning: See the most common words in a dataset before applying further processing.

Pro tips

  • Add domain‑specific stop words (e.g., "company", "product") to get cleaner results for niche content.
  • If you notice too many short words, bump up the minimum word length to 4 or 5.
  • For larger documents, the top 20 or 50 keywords usually give a great overview without noise.
  • After extraction, paste the keywords into our Word Counter or Keyword Density Checker for deeper analysis.
