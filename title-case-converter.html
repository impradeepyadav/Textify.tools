What is Title Case?

Title case is a capitalization style where the first letter of each word is capitalized. It is widely used for:

  • Headlines – blog posts, news articles, YouTube titles.
  • Book Titles – novels, research papers, e‑books.
  • Section Headings – inside documents, reports, and presentations.
  • Email Subjects – to make them stand out.
  • Social Media Captions – for a consistent, professional look.

AP Style vs. Standard Title Case

The tool offers two modes:

  • Standard Title Case – capitalizes EVERY word. Example: "The Quick Brown Fox Jumps Over The Lazy Dog".
  • AP Style – capitalizes only major words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) and leaves short words (a, an, the, and, of, for, in, on, at, by, to, with, from, up, down, out, off, over, under, again, then, when, where, why, how) in lowercase. Example: "The Quick Brown Fox Jumps over the Lazy Dog". The first and last word are always capitalized.

Standard capitalizes every word. AP Style skips short words (a, an, the, and, of, for, in, on, at, by, to, with, from, etc.) but always capitalizes the first and last word.
