Word Tips & Insights

Why track unique words? A high number of unique words often indicates richer vocabulary and less repetition. If your unique‑word count is close to your total word count, you're using a wide range of vocabulary. If it's low, you may be repeating yourself — try mixing in synonyms.

What's a good average sentence length? For most online content, aim for an average of 15–20 words per sentence. Shorter sentences improve readability; longer ones can make text feel dense. Use the average sentence length stat to gauge whether your writing is scannable.

How long should my blog post be? Studies show that posts between 1,500 and 2,500 words tend to perform well in search engines, but quality always beats quantity. Use the word counter to keep an eye on length while you draft.

Using top keywords to improve SEO: The top keyword stats show which words appear most often. If your main topic word isn't among them, you might need to emphasize it more. Conversely, if a filler word like "the" or "and" is #1, that's normal — the tool filters common stop words in some views, but for raw data it includes everything.

A Writer's Guide to Word Count

  • Social media captions: 50–150 characters (about 10–30 words). Short and punchy wins.
  • Blog posts: 1,000–2,000 words is the sweet spot for SEO, but tutorials and guides can go longer.
  • Essays & school papers: Follow the assigned word count — our tool helps you stay on track.
  • Email newsletters: Keep it under 500 words for the best open and click rates.
  • Speeches & presentations: At 130 words per minute, a 5‑minute talk needs roughly 650 words. Adjust the number in the speaking time stat to plan your script.

How Word Count Affects Different Types of Writing

Every format has its own "ideal" length, and straying too far can hurt your message. Here's a closer look at some common scenarios:

  • Landing pages: The best‑converting landing pages often use 300–500 words. Enough to persuade, not enough to bore.
  • Product descriptions: Aim for 100–300 words. Include key features, benefits, and a call to action without overwhelming the buyer.
  • Press releases: Keep it around 400–600 words. Journalists appreciate concise, fact‑packed announcements.
  • Cover letters: Stick to 200–400 words. Three to four paragraphs that show enthusiasm and fit — never a full page.
  • Resumes: Bullet points often beat paragraphs. Use the character count to keep each line sharp and under one line.

Word Count and SEO: Best Practices

Search engines love content that answers questions thoroughly. But more words don't always mean better rankings. Here's what actually matters:

  • Match search intent: If the top‑ranking pages for your keyword average 1,200 words, a 400‑word article probably won't compete — and a 3,000‑word one might be overkill unless it's truly better.
  • Break it up: Use headings, short paragraphs, and bullet points to make long posts scannable. Our average sentence length stat can help you keep paragraphs digestible.
  • Unique value: Don't pad word count with fluff. Use the unique‑word and top‑keyword data to spot over‑used terms and inject more meaningful vocabulary.
  • Internal linking: A longer post gives you more natural places to link to your other content. Just keep it relevant.
