What Exactly Are UTM Parameters?

Think of UTM parameters like tiny reporters that ride along with your links. When someone clicks, those reporters tell Google Analytics (or any other analytics tool) exactly which campaign, source, and medium sent that visitor. Without UTMs, traffic often gets dumped into the "direct" bucket, and you lose all insight into what's working.

utm_source (required)

The platform or site that sent the traffic. google, facebook, newsletter — you name it.

utm_medium (required)

The marketing channel. cpc for paid ads, email for newsletters, social for organic posts.

utm_campaign (required)

The specific campaign name, like black_friday or product_launch. This groups all your links together in reports.

utm_term (optional)

Mostly for paid search — tracks which keyword triggered the ad.

utm_content (optional)

Helps you A/B test different link placements or creative variations.

UTM Best Practices (A Quick Cheat Sheet)

  • Stick to lowercase. Google Analytics treats "Facebook" and "facebook" as two different sources. Save yourself the headache.
  • Use underscores or hyphens, not spaces. Spaces break URLs. utm_campaign=spring_sale works; utm_campaign=spring sale creates a mess.
  • Keep names short and consistent. Agree on a naming convention with your team — it prevents "fb", "facebook", and "Facebook" from splitting your data.
  • Don't UTM internal links. Only tag URLs that leave your own site. Tagging internal links can reset session data and distort your analytics.
  • Test before you blast. Generate the link, open it in a private window, and check that it lands where you expect. Takes five seconds, saves hours of confusion.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does UTM stand for?
UTM stands for "Urchin Tracking Module". Urchin was the company that originally developed the software that later became Google Analytics.
Are UTM parameters case‑sensitive?
Yes. In Google Analytics, "Facebook" and "facebook" are treated as different sources. Always use lowercase to avoid data fragmentation.
Can I use UTM parameters on any URL?
Yes, you can append UTM parameters to any valid HTTP/HTTPS URL. Avoid using them on internal redirect chains as they can break.
Is this tool private?
Absolutely. All encoding and URL generation happen inside your browser. Your base URL and UTM values are never sent to any server.
What if I leave the optional fields empty?
That's totally fine. Only utm_source, utm_medium, and utm_campaign are required. The optional fields are there when you need deeper tracking.
Why would I need a QR code for a UTM link?
QR codes bridge the gap between offline and online. Put them on flyers, posters, product packaging, or event booths — when scanned, the UTM parameters still report accurately in your analytics, so you can track offline efforts too.
