What is a URL Slug?

A URL slug is the part of a web address that identifies a particular page in a human‑readable format. For example, in example.com/blog/how-to-make-pasta, the slug is how-to-make-pasta. Slugs are essential for SEO because search engines use them to understand page content, and users find them easier to read and remember than random IDs.

Pro Tip: Keep slugs short (3‑5 words), use hyphens (not underscores), and include your main keyword. Avoid numbers unless they're part of the content (e.g., "2024‑review" is fine).

Slug Best Practices for SEO

  • Keep it concise: Aim for 3‑5 words. Remove fluff like "the", "a", "and".
  • Use hyphens as word separators: Google treats hyphens as space, while underscores join words together.
  • Include your target keyword: Place the most important term near the beginning of the slug.
  • Avoid dates and numbers: Unless the content is time‑sensitive, avoid years that make the URL look outdated.
  • All lowercase: Uppercase letters can lead to duplicate content issues on some servers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What characters are allowed in a slug?
In strict mode, only lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens. Without strict mode, the tool keeps alphanumeric characters and replaces spaces/special characters with hyphens.
Does the tool support non‑English characters?
Yes, but for best results, the tool transliterates accented characters (e.g., "café" becomes "cafe") to their ASCII equivalents. Full Unicode slugs may work in modern browsers but aren't recommended for SEO.
Is the slug generator free?
Absolutely. No signup, no limits, no premium tiers.
Does it work offline?
Once the page is loaded, generation works without an internet connection. All processing is client‑side.
